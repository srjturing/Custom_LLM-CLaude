B. Client Error Code Severities — score for every pair
B1. Relevance (R) — Major. 5 on‑topic/figure‑based; 3 minor tangent; 1 off‑topic/non‑visual claims.


B2. Question Complexity / Instruction Following (PIF) — Minor. 5 all instructions met; 3 small lapse; 1 misses required subplots/rounding directive.


B3. Comprehensiveness (CR) — Critical. 5 complete chain that teaches how to read; 3 small gap; 1 if plan/Approach missing, outer‑layer description missing, or chosen/excluded subplots not justified.


B4. Convention Following (CF) — Major. 5 conventions satisfied; 3 minor breach; 1 if any numeric reads occur before Execute, or fewer than 4 lines, or color‑only references.


B5. Factuality (F) — Critical. 5 all numbers/math align; 3 tiny rounding slip; 1 wrong value/formula misuse.


B6. Faithfulness to Provided Info (FRA) — Major. 5 entirely faithful; 3 slight over‑generalization; 1 unsupported claims.


How severities drive the verdict (single pair):
Any Critical (CR or F) at 1 ⇒ FAIL.


Any Major (R, CF, FRA) at 1 ⇒ usually FAIL (unless trivially fixable without touching facts → “Needs Rework” allowed).


Minor (PIF) at 1 ⇒ Needs Rework if isolated; escalate to FAIL if combined with any other 1.


Multiple 3’s across B1–B6 and Gatekeeper ⇒ Needs Rework.
